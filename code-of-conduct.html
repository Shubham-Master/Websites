Community Standards

Code of Conduct

Unmute works only when people treat each other with patience, consent, and care. This page sets the baseline for what protects the room.

Core idea

Safety is not just about stopping the worst behavior. It is also about shaping the kind of room where smaller harms do not quietly accumulate.

On this page

Respect Privacy Boundaries Harmful behavior How we respond

Respect the speaker

Let people finish. Do not interrupt, ridicule, interrogate, or compete with someone else's story.

Respect the room

What is shared in the room should stay there unless the speaker explicitly asks otherwise. Recording or redistributing personal stories breaks trust.

Respect boundaries

No one owes disclosure, camera use, explanation, or emotional labor. Consent matters in how you ask questions and how closely you move toward someone else's experience.

Harmful behavior is not tolerated

  • Harassment, intimidation, or repeated boundary-pushing
  • Discrimination or demeaning language
  • Advice-giving that overrides the room's format after being redirected
  • Attempts to expose or identify members who wish to remain private

How we respond

Facilitators and organizers may pause, redirect, remove, or ban participants whose behavior threatens the integrity or safety of the room.
